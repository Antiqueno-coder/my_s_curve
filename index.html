S-Curve Corporate Lifecycle Reference Manual (Annual Edition)
Phase 1: Early Acceleration Scale: 0% - 35% of Peak

Slow absolute revenue accumulation with high initial R&D and product incubation. Percentage growth is high, but dollar additions are small.

Phase 2: Mid-Inflection Transition Scale: 35% - 50% of Peak

Steep exponential climb. The business reaches its Inflection Point (maximum velocity midpoint) and unlocks maximum operating leverage.

Phase 3: Post-Inflection Decel Scale: 50% - 85% of Peak

Growth velocity mathematically transitions from acceleration to deceleration as addressable markets saturate and market penetration deepens.

Phase 4: Saturated Maturity Scale: 85%+ of Peak

The S-curve flattens out. Revenue scale operates close to carrying capacity (L). Further expansion relies on pricing power or secondary S-Curves.

                    Applied S-Curve Curve Modeling Formulas
                

                

                    

                        
                        1. 3-Parameter Logistic Model (Symmetric)
                        
y(t) = L / (1 + e^(-k * (t - t0)))

                        
Our default standard. Assumes growth is symmetric around a midpoint inflection ($t_0$) where the curve climbs steepest before flattening out toward carrying capacity ($L$), governed by scaling steepness ($k$). Unlike other curves, its symmetric path forces the inflection midpoint to occur at exactly 50% of peak capacity, assuming identical ramp-up and maturity deceleration rates.

                    

                    

                        
                        2. Gompertz Growth Model (Asymmetric)
                        
y(t) = L * e^(-e^(-k * (t - t0)))

                        
An alternative asymmetric model. Unlike the symmetric Logistic curve, a Gompertz curve assumes a company approaches its ultimate maturity ceiling ($L$) much slower than it departed from its origin. Unlike the symmetric logistic curve, its asymmetric design places the inflection point lower (at ~36.8%), mapping real-world markets that mature much slower than they initially scale.

                    

                    

                        
                        3. Bass Diffusion Model (Innovation/Imitation)
                        
f(t) = [p + q*F(t)] * [1 - F(t)]

                        
Models consumer product adoption rates based on two components: coefficient of innovation ($p$, early independent adopters) and coefficient of imitation ($q$, late-stage peer adopters copying early users). Unlike pure trend-fitting models, this equation explicitly splits customer adoption into separate innovator and imitator channels to model physical word-of-mouth market momentum.
